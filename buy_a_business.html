Where to buy a business

Flippa vs BizBuySell

When you're in the market to purchase an existing business, two platforms often rise to the top of the consideration list: Flippa and BizBuySell. Both serve as marketplaces for business acquisitions, but they cater to different segments of the market with distinct approaches. Let's explore what sets them apart.

Flippa:
The Digital Asset Marketplace

Flippa has carved out a niche as the go-to platform for digital businesses. It's particularly popular for e-commerce stores, content websites, apps, and other online ventures. The platform tends to feature more affordable options, with many listings under $50,000, making it accessible to first-time business buyers or those with limited capital.

The verification process on Flippa is relatively lightweight, which means you'll need to perform thorough due diligence. While this creates some additional work, it also means deals can move quickly - often closing within days or weeks rather than months.

Flippa's auction-style format creates a competitive environment where you might find yourself in bidding wars for attractive properties. However, this marketplace approach also means you might discover undervalued gems if you're patient and know what to look for.

BizBuySell:
The Established Business Broker

BizBuySell takes a more traditional approach to business sales. It primarily features brick-and-mortar businesses alongside established online operations, with higher average selling prices - often in the six to seven-figure range. You'll find everything from local restaurants and retail shops to manufacturing companies and professional service firms.

The platform functions more like a business broker network than an auction site. Listings typically involve business brokers who have vetted the businesses and prepared formal sales memorandums. This additional layer of professional involvement often results in more verified financial information but also typically comes with broker fees that add to the acquisition cost.

The sales cycle on BizBuySell is generally longer, with transactions sometimes taking months to complete. This extended timeline allows for more comprehensive due diligence but requires patience from potential buyers.

Which Platform Is Right For You?

The decision between Flippa and BizBuySell ultimately depends on your budget, timeline, and the type of business you're looking to acquire.

Choose Flippa if you're targeting digital assets, working with a smaller budget, or prefer a faster acquisition process. Just be prepared to roll up your sleeves for detailed due diligence.

BizBuySell might be the better option if you're in the market for an established business with verifiable revenue history, don't mind a longer purchase timeline, and have access to more substantial funding.

Many experienced business buyers actually monitor both platforms, recognizing that each serves a different segment of the market and that great opportunities can appear on either one. Whichever route you choose, remember that buying an existing business can be a complex process - take your time, do your homework, and consider working with legal and financial advisors to ensure a successful transaction.
